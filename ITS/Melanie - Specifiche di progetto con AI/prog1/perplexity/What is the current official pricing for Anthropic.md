# Under the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) as it stands in May 2026,

how are AI tools that analyze legal contracts for non-lawyers classified?
Specifically: are they considered high-risk under Annex III, limited-risk,
or minimal-risk? What changes if the tool is positioned as "decision-support"
with explicit disclaimers and human-in-the-loop, rather than as legal advice?
Cite the relevant Annex III categories and any 2025-2026 guidance from the
European Commission or national authorities.

**AI tools analyzing legal contracts for non-lawyers are typically classified as limited-risk or minimal-risk under the EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689) as of May 2026.** They do not match Annex III high-risk categories unless tied to administration of justice or decisions affecting rights.[^3_1][^3_2]

## Risk Classification

Annex III lists high-risk areas like (5) administration of justice and legal proceedings (e.g., systems assisting courts in interpretation or evidence evaluation); (4) access to/quality of essential services; and (2)(b) worker management. Pure contract analysis tools for non-lawyers fall outside these, landing in limited-risk (transparency obligations) or minimal-risk.[^3_3][^3_4][^3_1]

## Impact of Positioning

Positioning as "decision-support" with disclaimers ("not legal advice") and human-in-the-loop (HITL) invokes Article 6(3) derogations from high-risk: e.g., (a) narrow procedural task; (b) improves prior human activity; (c) detects patterns without replacing human assessment. This de-risks to limited/minimal if no significant influence on outcomes.[^3_5][^3_1]

## Guidance from Authorities

Article 6(5) requires Commission guidelines by February 2026 on classification with examples; as of May 2026, these clarify legal tech like CLM (contract lifecycle management) as limited/minimal absent Annex III ties. EDPS 2025 guidance emphasizes HITL for risk minimization. Legal tech analyses (e.g., JD Supra via Agiloft) confirm most contract review/summary tools avoid high-risk.[^3_2][^3_6][^3_4][^3_5]

# For an Italian-language contract analysis MVP that currently uses Claude

Sonnet 4 (~0.04 €/analysis, ~5K input + 2K output tokens), what alternative
LLM APIs in 2026 offer comparable or better performance on legal/structured-
extraction tasks at lower cost? Compare on: (1) price per million input/
output tokens, (2) Italian language quality, (3) JSON-mode/structured output
reliability, (4) data privacy terms (no-training-on-API-inputs guarantee),
(5) EU data residency options.
